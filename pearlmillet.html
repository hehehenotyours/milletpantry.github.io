Reasons to Include Pearl Millets in Your Diet

Pearl Millet (Bajra) - Pennisetum glaucum 
It has the largest kernels among all the varieties of millets. These can be nearly white, pale yellow, brown, grey, stale blue or purple.



1. Nutritional Benefits

Rich in Fiber (8 - 11.5 g/100g), High in Protein (11.8 g/100 g), Contains Iron (11 mg/100 g), Magnesium (97-137 mg/ 100 g), Phosphorus (230-280 mg/100 g), Low Glycemic Index, Contains Ferulic Acid (antioxidant)

2. Health Benefits

Supports digestion, prevents constipation, and aids in weight management. Suitable for people with gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Helps regulate blood sugar levels, making it ideal for diabetics. Reduces oxidative stress and supports immunity. Better blood pressure control and cardiovascular health. Reduces the risk of chronic diseases and cancer.

3. Textural Propoerties

The kernels are hard & dense. When milled, it produces a slightly coarse flour with grainy texture. After cooking, it develops a soft yet slightly chewy texture. Its texture adapts well to blending with other grains or binding Ideal for, Puffing, Grinding into flour, Steamed dishes, Porridges, Flat breads & Baking.
